The purpose of LEVIATHAN is to enable a community to congregate around the concepts that bind them. By doing so, they are able to better represent their core values, and to reconstruct those values over time, as required by changing circumstances.

For example, the introduction of a new community member may trigger some changes in the way the community works together; by opening up this experience to the audience - that is, the community itself - subjects may interact with observers to affect a positive change in the nature of the stage of the performance itself.

Or, more straightforwardly, you get to share and answer questions that are relevant to you and your community, and then see the effects of those answers upon the relationships between you and your peers.

This is useful, for example, when improving the workflow involved in a particular company task, or when developing a new tool for use in a workflow. Where the usual approaches like requirements gathering and user stories end - at the point where responsibilities hand off to those beyond the defined roles and requirements - the leviathan provides the opportunity to go a bit further, to see what everyone thinks of particular aspects of the activity in question, and to enable them to reconstruct their working practices as part of the process of changing the network in which they perform those practices by the aforementioned improvements or developments.

This service is actually just one instantiation of a leviathan - it is running the leviathan software. It is also possible, instead of submitting questions to this service, to run your own leviathan for your particular community or project. And you could call your version something less pretentious than leviathan, if you really want. The code is all open source, it is available at http://github.com/CottageLabs/leviathan.

The original Leviathan was a book written by Thomas Hobbes. The original cover art (now in the public domain) from that book can be seen in the background to this service. Read more about Hobbes and Leviathan.

If you want to read more about the research behind this service, have a look at Studies in Open Scholarship.

LEVIATHAN is supported by Cottage Labs. If you have a problem or a question, email us@cottagelabs.com.
